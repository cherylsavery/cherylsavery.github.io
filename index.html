About

I graduated in 2014 from Queen's University in Kingston, Ontario, Canada with a PhD in Computing. My research focused on consistency maintenance in networked games and I developed the Janus Toolkit that simplifies the implementation of a variety of game networking techniques.

I am currently working as an independent game developer. My goal is to develop games that are accessible to everyone. My games will focus on fun as well as thinking and planning as opposed to timing and quick reflexes.

I believe that my first game Coffee Crash is such a game. You swipe to move water drops and coffee beans to make cups of coffee. There is no time pressure to make your next move and careful planning is required to not lose your beans and water and to make groups of 4 or 5 coffees.
